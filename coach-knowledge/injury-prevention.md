# Injury Prevention

## Load Management

- ACWR is the primary injury predictor. Monitor weekly. See [[thresholds#Workload Ratio (ACWR)]] for exact zones and [[recovery-monitoring#ACWR (Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio)]] for interpretation.
- Spikes in training load (even positive ones like a race) increase injury risk for 7-14 days after.
- New movements or surfaces: introduce at 50% of expected volume and progress over 2-3 weeks.

## Red Flags — Stop the Session Immediately

1. Sharp, localized pain (not diffuse muscle soreness).
2. Pain that increases as the session continues.
3. Pain that alters movement pattern, gait, or stroke mechanics.
4. Numbness, tingling, or loss of strength in a limb.

## Yellow Flags — Modify and Monitor

1. Persistent soreness in one area lasting > 48 hours post-session.
2. Asymmetric fatigue (one leg significantly more tired than the other).
3. Joint stiffness that does not resolve after warm-up.
4. Sleep disruption caused by physical discomfort.

## Coaching Principle

When in doubt, shorten or skip the current session to protect the priority session later in the week. A lost easy day costs nothing. A lost key session or an injury costs weeks.
