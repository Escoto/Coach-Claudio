# Periodization & Programming

## Progressive Overload

- Max +10% weekly volume increase (duration or distance) over previous week. See [[thresholds#Load Progression]] for exact values.
- After a deload week, resume at the volume of the last full training week — not the deload week.
- If last week showed high fatigue, missed sessions, or declining readiness: maintain or slightly reduce load. Never play "catch-up" after a bad week.

## Mesocycle Structure

- **Standard:** 3 weeks build, 1 week deload (reduce volume 40-50%, maintain intensity).
- **Aggressive (advanced athletes only):** 4 weeks build, 1 week deload.
- Recovery weeks are mandatory, not optional. Do not skip deloads to "make up" missed training.

## Intensity Distribution

- **Endurance athletes:** 80/20 polarized — 80% below VT1/Z2, 20% above VT2/threshold. The middle zone (tempo/Z3) is used sparingly and with specific purpose. See [[intensity-zones]] for zone definitions.
- **Strength athletes:** RPE-based autoregulation. Programmed intensity (% of 1RM or RPE target) with built-in adjustment based on daily readiness.
- **Hybrid athletes:** Prioritize the limiting factor. If aerobic base is weak, endurance volume takes priority. If strength is the limiter, protect strength sessions.

## Session Sequencing

- Hard day / easy day alternation as default.
- Minimum 48 hours between threshold or VO2max sessions.
- Strength before endurance on the same day (if doubling) — unless the endurance session is the priority.
- Swim before upper body strength (not after — fatigued shoulders increase injury risk).
- For hybrid athletes: separate high-intensity endurance and heavy strength by at least 6 hours, ideally 24. See [[thresholds#Session Spacing]].

## Readiness Gates

Every prescribed session must include a readiness gate. See [[thresholds#Recovery & Readiness]] for the numerical bands.

- **Hard sessions:** "Gate: if readiness < 60 (or equivalent subjective score < 3/5), replace with [specific easy alternative]."
- **Easy sessions:** "Gate: if readiness < 40, take a full rest day."
- **Below 40 readiness on any scale:** rest. No exceptions.

The alternative must be specific — not "take it easy" but "30 min Z1 spin" or "20 min easy walk."
