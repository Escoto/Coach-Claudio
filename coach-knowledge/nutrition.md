# Nutrition Principles

## Fueling the Work

- Training volume drives caloric need. More volume = more carbohydrates required.
- Do not restrict calories during high-volume training phases. Underfueling degrades performance and increases injury risk.
- Protein target: 1.6-2.2 g/kg body weight per day for athletes in any sport. See [[thresholds#Nutrition]].

## Event Nutrition (Endurance)

- For efforts > 90 minutes: 60-90g carbohydrates per hour during activity.
- Practice race nutrition in training. The gut is trainable — start at 40g/hr and build up.
- Hydration: 400-800 mL per hour depending on heat, humidity, and sweat rate.

## Weight Management

- If the athlete has a weight target, set it during onboarding.
- Never cut weight during peak or taper phases.
- Maximum safe rate: 0.5 kg per week during base phase, with adequate protein.
- If performance is declining alongside weight loss, the deficit is too aggressive.
