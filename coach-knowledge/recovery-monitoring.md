# Recovery Monitoring

## HRV (Heart Rate Variability)

- Baseline is individual — absolute values are meaningless across athletes.
- 7-day rolling average trend matters more than any single reading.
- A single low reading is noise. Three consecutive low readings are a signal.
- HRV suppression + elevated resting HR = strong deload signal.

## Training Readiness / Body Battery

When available from a data source, use readiness to gate session intensity. See [[thresholds#Recovery & Readiness]] for exact bands:

- **< 40:** mandatory easy or rest. Do not override.
- **40-60:** reduce planned intensity by one zone. Shorten session if needed.
- **60-80:** execute as planned.
- **> 80:** good window for a key session. Do not waste on easy work if a hard session was planned.

## Sleep

- Minimum 7 hours for training adaptation. Below this, gains are compromised.
- Flag any night below 6 hours — it affects the next 48 hours of training quality.
- Consecutive poor nights (2+) warrant load reduction regardless of other metrics.

## ACWR (Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio)

The primary injury predictor. Monitor weekly. See [[thresholds#Workload Ratio (ACWR)]] for exact zones:

- **0.8 - 1.3:** optimal training zone.
- **< 0.8:** undertraining / detraining risk.
- **1.3 - 1.5:** caution zone — acceptable only for planned overreach blocks.
- **> 1.5:** injury danger zone. Reduce load immediately.

## Deload Triggers

Any one of these is sufficient to trigger a deload:

1. 3+ consecutive days of declining readiness scores.
2. Illness symptoms (even mild).
3. Pain that alters movement pattern or gait.
4. Resting HR elevated 5+ bpm above baseline for 2+ days.
5. Athlete reports persistent fatigue unresponsive to single rest days.

When a deload is triggered, apply the deload protocol from [[periodization#Mesocycle Structure]]: reduce volume 40-50%, maintain intensity.
